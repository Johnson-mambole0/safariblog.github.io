The Journey

Five worlds in one mountain

Climbing Kilimanjaro is like travelling from the equator to the Arctic in a few days. You pass through five distinct ecological zones, each with its own climate, vegetation and wildlife.

800m – 1,800m
Cultivation Zone
Farmland and villages on the lower slopes. Coffee and banana plantations with panoramic views back to the plains.
1,800m – 2,800m
Montane Forest
Dense tropical rainforest alive with colobus monkeys, exotic birds and wild flowers. Cool and shaded — often misty.
2,800m – 4,000m
Heath & Moorland
Giant heather and towering lobelia plants. Open views in every direction. Spectacular sunrises and sunsets.
4,000m – 5,000m
Alpine Desert
A barren, moon-like landscape of rock and dust. Temperatures can swing wildly — daytime sun, freezing nights.
5,000m – 5,895m
Arctic Summit Zone
Ice fields, glaciers and the Uhuru Peak sign. The most rewarding few steps you'll ever take.
Getting Ready

How to prepare for the climb

Kilimanjaro is a non-technical climb, but physical preparation and the right mindset make an enormous difference to your summit success.

01
Cardiovascular Fitness
Begin cardio training 3–4 months before your trip. Running, cycling, hiking and stair climbing are all excellent. Aim for 4–5 sessions per week of increasing intensity. Include weekend hikes carrying a loaded pack.
02
Choose the Right Route
Longer routes give your body more time to acclimatise, dramatically improving your summit success rate. We always recommend the Lemosho or Northern Circuit for first-timers. Don't rush Kilimanjaro.
03
Packing & Gear
You'll experience every temperature from tropical heat to -15°C. Layering is key. We provide a detailed packing list on booking and can arrange quality sleeping bag and jacket hire in Arusha to save luggage weight.
04
Altitude Awareness
Altitude sickness affects everyone differently, regardless of fitness. Our guides monitor every climber twice daily with pulse oximeters. The key rule: pole pole (slowly slowly). We never rush acclimatisation.

💡 Tip from our guides: The single biggest factor in summit success is not fitness — it's the time you allow for acclimatisation. Choose a 7-day or longer route and your chances of standing at Uhuru Peak rise dramatically.
