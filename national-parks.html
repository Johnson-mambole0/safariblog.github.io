Tanzania's National Parks

Seven extraordinary ecosystems — from the world's greatest wildlife migration to the last wild rhinos of East Africa.

The Parks

Seven worlds, one country

Tanzania's national parks cover an area larger than France. Each has a completely different character, wildlife and atmosphere — many travellers return again and again to explore them all.

UNESCO World Heritage Site
01
Serengeti
National Park

The Serengeti is the greatest wildlife spectacle on earth. Vast golden plains stretching to every horizon, home to the world's largest mammal migration — 1.5 million wildebeest, hundreds of thousands of zebra and gazelle moving in an ancient cycle of survival.

02
Ngorongoro
Crater

The world's largest intact volcanic caldera, Ngorongoro is often called the "Garden of Eden." The crater floor teems with an astonishing density of wildlife — lion, elephant, black rhino, flamingo and more — all within a natural bowl that feels like a lost world.

Elephant Country
03
Tarangire
National Park

Famous for its extraordinary elephant populations — some of the largest in Africa — Tarangire is characterised by ancient baobab trees and the meandering Tarangire River. In the dry season, animals crowd the river in incredible numbers, making it one of Tanzania's most productive game-viewing destinations.

Tree-Climbing Lions
04
Lake Manyara
National Park

A diverse and compact park running along the shores of an alkaline soda lake. Famous for its tree-climbing lions — a unique behaviour rarely seen elsewhere — and for the million-strong flamingo flocks that colour the lake pink. An ideal first or last stop on any northern circuit safari.

05
Arusha
National Park

Often overlooked in favour of its famous neighbours, Arusha National Park punches well above its weight. Nestled around Mount Meru, it offers walking safaris, canoeing on Fig Tree Crater Lake, and excellent giraffe and buffalo sightings — all within an hour of Arusha city.

Flamingo Breeding Ground
06
Lake Natron
& Ol Doinyo Lengai

One of Africa's most otherworldly landscapes. The blood-red alkaline lake is the world's primary flamingo breeding ground, and the adjacent Ol Doinyo Lengai volcano — the "Mountain of God" — is one of the most active in Africa. A truly off-the-beaten-path destination.
