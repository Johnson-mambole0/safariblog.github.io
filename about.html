Our Story

From Arusha, for the world

We started with a single Land Cruiser, two guides, and an unshakeable belief that Tanzania's wildlife and mountains deserved to be shared with the world — honestly, sustainably, and with genuine care for every person who came to us.

Over 12 years later, we remain exactly what we set out to be: a Tanzania-based company, staffed almost entirely by Tanzanians, that puts quality and authenticity above all else. We have never opened an office outside the country and we never will.

Our guides are the best in the business — many have been with us since the beginning. They know the Serengeti's lion prides by name, the best camp spots on Kilimanjaro, and exactly where to find the cheetah at dusk on the short grass plains.

Every trip we run is planned by people who have done it themselves, guided by people who live here, and supported by a community that depends on responsible tourism to thrive.

What We Stand For

Our values

These aren't marketing words. They are the commitments that shape every decision we make, every hire we offer and every trip we run.

Rooted Locally

99% of our staff are Tanzanian nationals. We hire locally, train locally, and reinvest in the communities that make our work possible. We believe the best guides are the ones who grew up in the places they show you.

Safety Without Compromise

Every guide is certified in wilderness first aid. Every mountain team carries emergency oxygen. We never skip a briefing, never rush acclimatisation, and never put group pressure above individual safety. Ever.

Conservation-Led

We contribute to ranger anti-poaching programmes, fund tree-planting initiatives around Kilimanjaro, and partner with community conservancies. Tourism must give back more than it takes — that is not negotiable for us.

Honest Pricing

We do not cut corners to win price wars. Our prices reflect fair wages for our entire team — guides, porters, cooks and camp staff. When you travel with us, you know your money supports real livelihoods.

Our Impact

Protecting what we love

Tanzania's wild places are irreplaceable. We have a responsibility — and a privilege — to protect them. Every year we invest a portion of our revenue directly into conservation and community projects.

We plant trees on the degraded southern slopes of Kilimanjaro, support anti-poaching ranger patrols in the Serengeti, and fund bursaries for children in the communities we operate in.

Our guiding principle: every traveller should leave Tanzania in better shape than they found it.
